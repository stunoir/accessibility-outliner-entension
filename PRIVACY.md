# Privacy policy - Accessibility Outliner

Last updated: 31 May 2026

Accessibility Outliner does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data.

## What the extension accesses

When you click one of its tools, the extension reads the content of the page you are currently viewing, solely to find and visually outline accessibility issues (such as undersized controls, empty headings, links with no accessible name, and duplicate IDs). It reads the page only at the moment you click a tool, and it discards that information immediately after outlining the issues. It never reads any page in the background.

## What the extension stores

The only data the extension stores is its own popup state: which tool was last run and the issue count for each tab. This is kept in your browser's session storage (`chrome.storage.session`), which is local to your device, cleared when the browser closes, and never synced to Google or sent anywhere.

## What the extension does not do

- It makes no network requests.
- It contains no analytics, tracking, or advertising.
- It sends nothing off your device.
- It does not sell or share any data with third parties.

## Permissions

The extension requests only `activeTab` and `scripting` (to run a check against the page you are viewing, when you click a tool) and `storage` (to remember the popup's state per tab). It declares no host permissions.

## Contact

If you have any questions about this policy, contact the developer at the email address listed on the extension's Chrome Web Store listing.
